Questions and answers

Ask a question

  • Who can publish a project?

    Anybody can publish a project. You can be a registered NGO (non-governmental organisation), other organisation or a single local actor taking on a project. As a PUBLISHER you register yourself and prepare your project. We will give you all our support in doing that. When you are ready, you publish your project and launch your pledge for support.

  • How does it work with money?

    Every project publisher sets their project's funding goal (support needed to get the project going) and deadline. If SUPPORTERS like the project, they can pledge money to make it happen. If the project succeeds in reaching its minimum funding goal (typically less then 100% when scalability of needs apply; we help to identify it depending on the type of a project), all supporters' credit cards are charged by the set deadline. If the project falls shorter than that, no one is charged. If a project is successfully funded, LocalActors.org applies a 4% fee to the funds collected.

  • What is my guarantee?

    There is a high degree of transparency in publishing the project and feedback on progress. At the same time all community can post their comments and references in the visitors' book of each project. Your guarantee is the community. Good cause projects can start small and grow. We recommend taking all projects step by step and phasing the funding needed. We give each project dedicated support in planning and monitoring. Your guarantee is the direct link between the supporter and the project.

  • What kind of projects can I submit?

    We support local action and we are willing to publish good cause - non-for-profit - projects to help them get off the ground. Let it be a school coach trip, a new dwell in a village or a new service to help local community. You can submit any projects in line with our vision and in need of little support in financing, knowledge or materials to get things going.

  • Why should I choose Local Actors for my project?

    If you have a project, through LocalActors.org you can address small donors directly and world-wide to get fundings or other forms of support. Few of such websites exist, but none is focusing on local non-for-profit projects while giving a chance to wide range of set-ups. LocalActors.org puts you in contact with a large pool of passionate supporters and helps you market your idea.

  • When will the service be available?

    Currently LocalActors.org founding team and supporters are gathering first projects and getting in touch with a kick-off community of users. During the early spring a beta-test with self-registration of projects on the website will be held and in Spring will be going live! In the meanwhile you can already submit a project. LocalActors.org will process it, share it with different supporters and your project will be among the first being published.

  • How can I support?

    If you want to support a particular local project, you can choose to do so either financially, with human resources or your skills.

  • What do I get from being a supporter?

    We start as a small group of those not happy with the options opened for small donors - little transparency, no feedback and no visibility of what my contribution has done. As a supporter to the local action we will make sure you will have all of that, letting you know where exactly the support goes and which specific benefits it brings.

Think about your project

Requirements, benefits and guidelines

What is your goal? What do you want to achieve and when? How much it will costs and what other support you may need (perhaps, some materials, teachers etc.)? Keep it simple, keep it phased. Plan for a year and say what you achieve in each quarter. Project of 500 EUR will be easier to fund than a project of 2000 EUR. Think through what you need most.

EXAMPLE: If you want to take 40 kids on a day coach trip, explain why they should go - what they get from the trip, how your choose the 40, when is the trip expected to take place and calculate the price of the trip (coach with the driver or also anything else?)
